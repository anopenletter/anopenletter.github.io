An open letter to Jasper Saito, wherever he may be
Hey Jasper.



It's Riki.
I know, I know. My name probably doesn`t mean much to you.



But trust me, my goofy grin or human stench wouldn’t mean a whole lot to you either.
We’ve never met. But in a funny sort of way, I feel like I know you.
Is it odd that I’m nervous meeting you? Even in this indirect way?



Even knowing that these words may never leave the confines of my journal, it’s hard not to wonder what you’d make of me.
Probably that I’m a very terrible dog with very little fur and who’s really not all that good at fetch.
But would you like me anyway?
I should mention…. We do have one thing in common.



Something that ties the ribbons of our stories together, across both space and time.

He’s my favourite. And I’ve a sneaky suspicion he might be yours too.
It's Josh.
That picture of him on the log, giggling at my attempt at seesawing? That’s Josh on his 22nd birthday, the day after we’d spent the night freezing our tiny asses off, lying in the snow, trying to start a fire, in a closed-for-the-winter section of Bruce Peninsula National Park with what can only be described as very inappropriate camping gear. I know, I know. A typical Josh story, right?
