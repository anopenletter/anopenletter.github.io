An open letter to Jasper Saito, wherever he may be
Hey Jasper.


It's Riki.
I know, I know. My name probably doesn`t mean much to you.


But trust me, my goofy grin or human stench wouldn’t mean a whole lot to you either.
We’ve never met. But in a funny sort of way, I feel like I know you.
Is it odd that I’m nervous meeting you? Even in this indirect way?


Even knowing that these words may never leave the confines of my journal, it’s hard not to wonder what you’d make of me.
Probably that I’m a very terrible dog with very little fur and who’s really not all that good at fetch.
But would you like me anyway?
I should mention…. We do have one thing in common.


Something that ties the ribbons of our stories together, across both space and time.

He’s my favourite. And I’ve a sneaky suspicion he might be yours too.
That video of him on the log, giggling at my attempt at seesawing?


That’s Josh on his 22nd birthday, the day after we’d spent the night freezing our tiny asses off, lying in the snow, trying to start a fire, in a closed-for-the-winter section of Bruce Peninsula National Park with what can only be described as very inappropriate camping gear.


I know, I know. A typical Josh story, right?

It’s funny, I can just picture you lying on the ground, your eyebrow half-raised as this unfamiliar human fumbles through his introduction at you.


But then, at the mention of Josh, I sense your fur standing on edge, your head picking up off the ground, your ears perking up, and your eyes darting about the room to see if he’s around, with your tail wags firmly on standby.

Maybe you were hoping he’d come to take you on another hike.


Or a casual stroll around the neighbourhood just one more time.


Or perhaps that you’d both hop in the family car, sleeping away those long summer night drives home with your head in his lap.
But I`m sorry. He’s not here.
It’s just me.


But I do have messages for you.
He still talks about you. All the time actually. That’s how I first heard of you: in all the stories he tells about you.
And when he does, his eyes immediately light up, his speech quickens, and he jumps erratically from topic to topic like I never see him do otherwise, all while giddily smiling into his chest, giggling as you come bounding back to him through the blue mist of his memories.
Once, while touring Josh’s campus, hand in hand, for almost an hour he regaled how one summer at the old house, he spent weeks building a little outhouse for himself, complete with fairy lights and little plants and plush cushions.


He measured it up, calculated it all out, double-checked all the gear ratios (or whatever it is that architects check…), and headed, list in hand, to Canadian Tire for the plywood he needed.

It was that summer Josh learnt that 2x4s are not ACTUALLY 2x4.


Like most masculine measurements, they’re a little inflated, and 2x4s in reality are actually more like 1.5x3.5s.
You probably witnessed that realisation.


You probably saw Josh at first puzzled as he laid out all the sparingly-acquired wood, and then him suddenly kicking the turf and tossing a 1.5x3.5 across the lawn, lifting your head from the grass quizzingly.
Wow, you must have thought, Josh really loves huge sticks!


We have so much in common!
And then I imagine you running around in circles after your own tail as Josh grumpily heads inside to grab your leash, resigned to a second trip to Canadian Tire.
None of that really mattered though.


All those little mistakes? They were all just part of the experience. Part of the learning curve of the summertime project he’d embarked upon with you by his side, as you jumped through piles of raked leaves and watched him do whatever strange thing he was doing now and tried clandestinely to drag away pieces of wood two times your size.


After all, why would you lay all that wood down and just walk over it when you could carry it about in your mouth and show everyone?!
I know you caught what I said though; I saw the ever-so-slight tilt of your head as I said it.
But I didn`t misspeak.


Yes, we were hand in hand.
Josh and I are dating.


In fact, those videos of him I showed you earlier? Those are from the day we first expressed our love for one another.
I like to think that when you hear that your tail begins to wag uncontrollably.
I think of you as the first person Josh ever came out to.


Perhaps not in so many words, but I know you knew. You could sense that small sadness he sometimes carried around with him, and you comforted him more than perhaps you’ll ever know.
But more than needing someone to tell him what to think or feel, you knew he just needed time.


So you loved and respected and supported him regardless of what he wore, or who he hung out with, or how many Facebook likes he had, or his sexuality, and, for your part, you never told another soul his secrets or expected him to be anything more than who he is.


And for that, I owe you a million thank yous.
But he’s out now. At least partially. At university in Waterloo, Josh lives as an out gay man.


He even works for his university’s LGBTQ+ society, supporting others the way he once needed support. The way you supported him.
Like me, I know you`d be so proud of him.


This year, he didn`t just attend Pride, something he had feared doing for many years, but he helped to rally, organise, promote and even escort people right into its loving arms.
And he stood atop his university float alongside his best friend and other Glow volunteers and he danced his little heart out to the music, posing for pictures like a ~~22~~-year old model.


It was beautiful. He was beautiful.
The family don’t know yet, but they will. I know he’ll tell them eventually.


He has the strength, but it just hasn’t been the right time yet. And when he does tell them, he might miss your unwavering love and support doubly that night.
But please don’t be alarmed by that news.


No matter what happens going forward, Josh knows that I and many of the other beautiful souls he calls friends will always offer our unflinching support.


There may be bumps along the way to his family's understanding and embracing of his sexuality, none of us can promise with certainty that there won't be, but they’ll be ok. I know it.


And when or if things are ever rough, there’ll always be a warm, loving space for him to curl up in with me, the way he once did with you.
On one of our first dates, he took me to Coquitlam to show me the neighbourhood you must know so well.


At one site, he parked his car on the incline of a small hill, and we entered a clearing between the surrounding trees. A short way in, we came across a small bridge atop a creek, and after standing on its boards a while to appreciate the view, we clambered down a little mud path, and embraced beside the bank.


The sky was a brilliant purple and the stars were just beginning to faintly twinkle, the tall pines silhouetted in a gradient from forest green to inky black.
It was then that he told me he often visited that creek with you.


We held each other a little longer, and as I stood feeling the warmth of his embrace and the steady I am, I am brag of his heart, I became ever more entranced by him.


A gust of wind teased at one of my trouser legs, and for a moment I thought it was you, brushing past us on your way back from the creek with some small rocks in your mouth. Because he told me that too. The way you’d try to save the rocks, gathering them a few at a time in your mouth, and bringing them from the waterside up to higher ground, eventually curating a little pile of your own, only now at a safer distance.
And as we finished our short ascent to leave, we turned back to look at the place where we just stood wrapped in one another, and noticed a diminishing but still evident pile of rocks a few feet from where we’d embraced.


Jasper… Know that you may be gone, but your legacy lives on in so many ways.
It could only have been more magical had you been there with us, your fur matted and clumpy from splashing about in the water.


I know you like to get muddy; I’ve seen the pictures of you that Josh posted on Facebook. I even sketched one of them once in Central Park during Josh and I’s early days.


I knew even then how much you mean to Josh, and I was terrified to capture you even slightly wrong, so I scoured entire swathes of New York for a library that would agree to let me print your picture without a library card, and sat intently studying your face before every pen stroke.
Josh has yet to see that sketch. I’ve promised him that once the entire sketchbook is complete, the whole thing is his to keep.


(Even if it is a little waterlogged from that one time I trusted Josh to water Bruce…


But that’s a story for another time.)


In that picture, you’re sitting on the floor of a car, your underside and face covered entirely in mud, and you’re panting and smiling up at Josh from behind his camera lens.
That night back at the creek, we left the woods in almost complete silence, Josh wrapped around my arm, both of us looking up and marvelling at the beauty of the world we share.


I think it was then that I knew I’d wait for him while he toured Australia.


I think it was then that I knew for sure that I was with someone pretty special. And I know you’d agree.
But I guess you’ve yet to hear about Australia either.
A few weeks after we met, Josh left Canada Australia-bound for almost three months with his best friend Keisha.


Things between us then were so uncertain. Going into our first few dates, neither of us expected a thing, but having been blind-sided by the lure of each other’s magic, there we stood, on the day Josh was due to depart, not knowing what to say or do.


I hoped there’d be a Chapter 2 to our story, but that’s all I could do. Hope.
I knew I’d wait for him to come back that December, cancelling plans to head home to England for the holidays, but at that point it was still uncertain if we’d even still be talking after 2 months apart.


So much between us rested on just a few dates in Vancouver.
Josh helped rescue sea turtles, and drove around the Australian coast in a van, and attended Pride, and made coffee in women’s trousers to support his trip, and ate mangoes he had picked in the wild, and drank wine in a restaurant parking lot with the middle-aged owner, and scuba dived amid pristmatic coral jungles, and so many other incredible moments.


I'm sure many of the memories Josh made on that trip he'll carry with him fondly for life.
And I know he would have loved to have shown you it all. Every single bit of it.
And his growth didn’t just finish there.


Since getting back to Canada, he’s started working at a local coffee shop to fund his education...
...and he’s resolved himself to drastically reducing his plastic waste and general environmental footprint...
...and he’s become a vegan to limit the suffering caused by his dietary choices...
...and he still swims fairly regularly.


There’s no need to freak out as you read that! I know you were always scared when he’d get in the water, worried those noodly arms won’t carry him far, but I promise he’s a great swimmer! He can even do laps and everything!
All silliness aside, Josh has really grown into a man that I know you’d be incredibly proud of.
Swimming might be one of only a few times Josh ever takes his necklace off.


Did you ever see it? It’s a silver chain with a cross pendant from his aunt, and it bears your name.


For as long as I’ve known him, he rarely takes it off, and even if he does, it’s only ever for a short while.
...except that one time when I accidentally pocketed it and only found it again as I boarded my flight away from Josh in Ontario all the way to England.


I wasn’t due to fly back for another whole week, and I was terrified the entire time that I might lose it. I felt like I was carrying you around with me, and if I lost it, Josh might never forgive me for having lost you.


I didn’t want him to lose you twice.
But I took you with me everywhere I went that trip, out on little excursions with me through my hometown of London city, and the next morning, I’d always send Josh an update over breakfast with all the pictures I had taken of you and I in the place where I grew up.
Behind the camera though? The true behind the scenes?


Before each picture, I’d tightly wind the necklace several times around my fingers and grip it as tightly as possible. There was no way I was letting you go anywhere. And once I had the picture, you’d go straight back into the safest pocket in my bag, and I’d never open it for any reason...except to check 30 more times that day that you were still in there and safe.
And I didn’t lose you, and a week later, I delivered you safely back to Josh.


He’s probably wearing it right now. Wherever he may be right now, whatever he may be doing, I bet you he’s wearing it.
But I’ve said a lot already, and I still have yet to tell you why I’m even writing to you at all.


Allow me to explain.
Josh and I just attended a music festival together in downtown Toronto.


It was our first festival experience together, and it was truly magical. Getting all glittered up in our tiny hostel bathroom, downing the cheapest drinks from some bougie LCBO, getting a little too tipsy in a restaurant alleyway to avoid the cops, confusing the hell out of our Uber driver, being young and wild and fluorescent and completely free beside one another, dancing with each other as the music pulsed, and the occasional moment where one or both of us would lean over for a kiss.
When the festival was over and the music died, Josh and I casually wound our way through downtown Toronto, towards the bus station. The beauty of our time together was still tingling its way through my body, but the fun was over and it was time for us to return to real life.


Josh had class the next day and needed to make it back to Waterloo that night.


We stood on a bridge for a while overlooking downtown, appreciating the cityscape as it shimmered and ebbed. That day was the anniversary of your death. June 3rd.
Josh called his parents who were on vacation at the time and then, right after, he called Colton. Colton hadn’t called them in a while; Josh reminded him to that night.


That’s your legacy, Jasper. Even in your absence, even as time goes by, you’re still bringing the family together.
As Josh spoke to the family, I walked soberly beside him, overhearing every word as it reverberated through the quiet Sunday night air.


Occasionally, Josh would look up and notice that I seemed deep in thought, and would reach out to squeeze my hand.


Truth be told, I think he thought I was upset at being left out of the conversation.
But that wasn’t it all... I was completely awestruck.


Jasper, I don’t think it’s possible for a family to have loved someone more than they loved you.


I know that they haven’t fully processed your parting still. I know that thinking about you still brings them a lot of pain, but you bring them together in such a pure way. And you remind them to put aside their differences, and strifes, and to find some time in their busy schedules to come back together and heal, replenish, restore.
They’re doing amazingly by the way.
Colton just came to visit Josh in Ontario for the first time and, as they parted, Josh called me, bawling his eyes out and waving his brother goodbye. Josh had been so excited to show his brother the life he had carved out for himself in Waterloo, hoping to inspire Colton to do the same in his own little corner of the world.


I know they didn’t often see eye to eye, and I’m sure you saw a lot of their childhood disputes, but I want you to know that they’re closer than ever, and, over time, I know their relationship is only going to blossom.
Colton starts university himself in the fall.
And Ross is still exercising and participating in triathlons and attempting in his own way to ensure that Josh and the rest of the family are successful and cared for and happy.


And you’ll never guess what! Despite all that trouble he used to give Josh, he’s only now become vegan!


I know! Josh rolled his eyes too.
And Andrea is still as blissfully happy as ever, guiding the family with her great kindness and warmth and consideration.


Josh and her are still extremely close. It’s a relationship that I’ve never known, and it’s incredibly beautiful to witness.
I wish they got to speak more often, but life has a way of getting you wrapped up.


If Josh ever does read this, perhaps one day when idly rummaging through my things, or if I ever choose to share it with him in some capacity, I hope that when it's over, he’ll call his mom.


Just for no reason… Just to say hi… Just to see what she’s up to.
Our time with our loved ones is a precious and fleeting thing.
And so there I stood, and there I walked, and there I listened to this amazing family come together around their collective love and mourning for a single person: You.


Jasper Saito.

But Josh never did make that bus.


We sat on a dock near the bus station looking out across the water, speaking so deeply and openly and comfortably with one another. When it came time for us to part, we drifted idly in the direction of the bus station….only to find that it was actually a 30-minute walk away.


Josh had got the location wrong.

We immediately, and in unison, clutched the stuff in our pockets and slammed the pavement.


We didn’t make it. Breathless, Josh breezily resigned himself to staying with me on our hard hostel mattress for another night, and we giggled the whole way back.


By the time we got to the orange glow of our streetlamp lit room, my face ached from a whole evening spent giggling.

In the morning, Josh left early for Waterloo, and I got ready for a job interview later that day.

I couldn't sleep. It's true that I was nervous, but I was also thinking about Josh and his family and their conversation about you.

It just wouldn’t leave my thoughts. Clouding everything in that familiar blue mist.

I made my way to a coffee house to await my interview, and wrote about five different drafts of this letter to you.


They all sucked, and I hastily scribbled them all out not long after writing them.
It wasn’t long before my alarm sounded and I was pulled away from my thoughts, tossing my pen and journal back into my bag, and dashing through the streets of Toronto towards Sick Kids Hospital.


The interview was a success! But then came time for the long haul back to Josh.
Josh lives several hours outside of Toronto by transit, and, thinking I was smart, I designed an alternate route to the one Josh had shown me.


A few hours later, I was stuck in the middle of nowhere with no cellphone reception, waiting for a bus in a remote location that had very little signage, hoping that a bus would eventually appear and that I wouldn’t just end up the saddest little city boy stranded in the middle of Ontario’s farmland.
I had 20 minutes until it was due to arrive, so I reached into my bag, pulled out my pen, and my journal, and once again embarked upon a letter to you.


That time the letter stuck, and that is where I was when this letter began.
Now? Now I’m seated at the back of the bus, cursing their broken outlets and my subsequent inability to charge my phone, but grateful that it came at all, and grateful that I’m hurtling at high speeds through the obsidian roadways back to Josh’s arms.
It had been a beautiful day but it was cooling to night as I waited for the bus, and the clear summer sky had turned an amethyst purple.


As I sat down on the pavement, using my knees as a writing desk, the heavens opened, and a light rain began to fall.

And that’s when I remembered it.


That’s when I remembered a French word I learnt years ago in my childhood. And that I had loved but since forgotten.
Serein. A word meaning light rainfall from a cloudless sky after sunset.
Serein. A deep feeling of serenity, calm and peace.
Serein. The peculiar mixture of bliss and nostalgia we feel when thinking of loved ones past.
Serein. The light rain from a cloudless sky that falls over Josh, and Andrea, and Ross, and Colton ever since the sun set on Jasper Saito.
I hope you know just how hard they tried to save you.
They researched alternate treatments, attended workshops, read article after article, desperate for more hope than they were being offered.
Josh started an Instagram campaign to find you a suitable stem cell donor.
Andrea reached out to her local newspaper to help spread the word of your plight.
But despite their best efforts, there wasn’t much time, and the lymphoma spread.
I know exactly how that feels. The helplessness. The fear. The sadness.


My father died of cancer too. Died the very day he was due to start treatment.
Actually, my whole family carry a mutation that makes us prone to cancer, and I am no exception.


Perhaps when my time is up, cancer too shall come to claim me, as it did you, and my father, and I can only hope that in the time I’m allowed I’ll have impacted the lives of the people around me the way you impacted the lives of the Saito family.
Soon I’m going to say goodbye, and perhaps we’ll never speak again.


But I have one last message before I go.
I think spirits like yours come into the world to teach people something.


And you were exactly what each of the Saitos needed exactly when they needed it.


They might have been the ones to teach you tricks, but you taught them a whole lot more. And even in your absence, within the negative space you've left, you can still see Jasper Saito, bringing the family together, and carried around in the lessons you taught, held nine moons beneath their heart.
You taught them how to love without judgment.
You taught them how to live without restraint.
To this day, you still mean so much to Josh.


You were his closest friend for a long time, before I even knew his name.
And he shall continue growing in new and unexpected ways, and I hope to witness and support and promote that journey.


But Jasper Saito will always be leaping through the blue mist of his memories, reminding him to approach life with inexhaustiable excitement and compassion.
So I stand before you at last and say...
Thank you, Jasper. Thank you for looking after Josh for all those years.


And thank you for helping him become the man I now get to know.
We love you.
And I know I’m a terrible dog with very little fur and who’s bad at fetch.


But do you like me anyway?
And before I go...
...just one last thing...
...one tiny, insignificant question that I’ve been burning to ask you...
...I was just wondering...
What kind of horse are you?!
